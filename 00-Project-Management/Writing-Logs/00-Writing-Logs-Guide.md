# Writing Logs Guide

Writing logs are a daily or session-based record of your progress, decisions, and observations during the book project. They serve as a project journal that helps you track momentum, document key decisions, and maintain continuity between writing sessions.

## Purpose

Writing logs help you remember what you were thinking during each session, track word count progress, document research leads and dead ends, record decisions about structure or argument, and note questions that need follow-up.

## How to Use

1. Create a new log file for each writing session using the [[Writing-Log-Template]]
2. Name it with the format: `YYYY-MM-DD-Writer-Log.md`
3. Tag with `#pm/workflow #team/author #doc/note`
4. Fill in session goals before you begin writing
5. Update progress, challenges, and next steps as you work or at the end of the session
6. Link to any chapters, sources, or contacts referenced during the session

## Tips

- Be honest about challenges — future-you will thank present-you for the context
- Note any research rabbit holes, even if they seem tangential — they often become useful later
- Record word counts to track momentum over time
- If working with an AI assistant, note what prompts and approaches were effective
