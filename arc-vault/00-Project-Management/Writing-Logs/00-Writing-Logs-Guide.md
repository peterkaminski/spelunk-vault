# Writing Logs Guide

Tags: #doc/guide #pm/workflow #team/all

## Purpose

Writing logs serve as a record of your writing sessions, documenting progress, decisions, challenges, and insights. They help track the development of your work over time and provide context for future reference.

## When to Create a Log

Create a writing log for each significant writing session. This might be daily, weekly, or per major milestone depending on your workflow. The key is consistency rather than frequency.

## Log Format

Use the template from `99-Templates/Writing-Log-Template.md` to create new logs. Name your log files using the format: `YYYY-MM-DD-[Writer/Team]-Log.md`

Examples:
- `2026-01-28-Sarah-Log.md`
- `2026-01-28-Story-Team-Log.md`
- `2026-01-28-Volume-02-Log.md`

## What to Include

Each log should capture the essential information about your writing session:

**Session Goals** document what you planned to accomplish, helping you stay focused and providing a measure for evaluating progress.

**Progress** describes what was actually completed, including word count changes or pages written. Be specific about chapters, scenes, or documents worked on.

**Challenges** record any difficulties encountered, whether plot problems, character issues, or technical obstacles. This helps identify patterns and informs future planning.

**Decisions Made** document important creative choices made during the session. These decisions may affect continuity and should be referenced when updating the series bible.

**Next Steps** outline what to work on in the next session and any preparation needed. This helps you start the next session with clear direction.

**Related Content** links to chapters, character notes, world-building documents, or other materials referenced during the session. This creates a web of connections across your vault.

## Tagging

Always include these tags:
- `#pm/workflow` - Identifies this as a workflow document
- `#team/writing` - Indicates it's writing team content
- `#doc/note` - Categorizes it as a note document

Add volume-specific tags if working on a particular volume:
- `#vol/01` - If working on Volume 1
- `#vol/02` - If working on Volume 2

## AI Assistant Logs

If using AI assistants for writing or brainstorming, include additional information:
- Prompts used
- Generation settings or parameters
- Which AI system was used
- How the generated content was modified or integrated

## Using Logs for Reflection

Periodically review your writing logs to identify patterns, track progress over longer periods, and reflect on your creative process. Logs can reveal:
- Productivity patterns and optimal writing times
- Recurring challenges that need systematic solutions
- Evolution of ideas and creative decisions
- Progress toward milestones and goals
