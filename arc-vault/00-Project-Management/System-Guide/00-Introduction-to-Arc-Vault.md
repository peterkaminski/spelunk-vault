# Arc Vault System Guide

This guide explains how to effectively use the Arc Vault system for multi-volume novel series development and manuscript management.

Arc Vault is an opinionated framework with specific recommendations and guidelines. Feel free to customize the framework to make it fit your team and its needs.

## Core Principles

The Arc Vault system is built on five foundational principles that guide its organization and use. **Clear Organization** ensures that every file has its place in the hierarchy, making it easy to locate materials regardless of project complexity. **Version Control** enables teams to track changes and development over time, preserving the evolution of ideas and decisions. **Continuity** is essential for multi-volume works, maintaining consistency across volumes in characters, world-building, and plot threads. **Accessibility** provides easy navigation and cross-referencing through wikilinks and tags, allowing writers to quickly find related content. Finally, **Flexibility** allows the system to adapt to changing needs while maintaining its core structure.

## Key Components

The system consists of four key components working together. The **Folder Structure** organizes content by purpose and stage in the writing process, from initial concepts through final manuscripts. The **Tagging System** enables cross-referencing and categorization of content across different areas of the vault. **Templates** maintain consistency across documents and provide starting points for common content types. **Workflows** establish standard processes for common tasks like moving content from development to manuscript stage or updating canonical information.

## Getting Started

To begin using Arc Vault effectively, start by reviewing the tagging system in [[02-Tags]] to understand how content is categorized and cross-referenced. Familiarize yourself with the folder structure detailed in [[01-File-Structure]] to know where different types of content belong. When creating new content, use templates from `99-Templates/` to maintain consistency. Follow established workflows documented in [[03-Workflows]] for your specific role on the team.

## Navigation Tips

Effective navigation relies on several practices. Use tags for cross-referencing content across different areas of the vault. Link related documents using the double-square-bracket `[[Page Name]]` wikilink convention, which works seamlessly in Obsidian. Maintain the series bible in `10-Series-Bible/` as your canonical reference. Track character and plot arcs in `60-Maps/` to visualize connections across volumes. Keep continuity notes updated in each volume's folder to maintain consistency.

## Multi-Volume Considerations

Writing a multi-volume series requires special attention to continuity and long-term planning. Character development must be tracked across volumes to ensure consistent growth and change. Plot threads may span multiple volumes and need careful tracking to ensure proper setup and payoff. World-building details must remain consistent throughout the series. The series timeline should be maintained to avoid chronological errors. Volume-to-volume transitions need planning to maintain reader engagement while advancing the overall story arc.
